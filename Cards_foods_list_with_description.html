Veg Salad

Chicken Curry

A typical curry from the Indian subcontinent consists of chicken stewed in an onion- and tomato-based sauce, flavoured with ginger, garlic, tomato puree, chilli peppers and a variety of spices, often including turmeric, cumin, coriander, cinnamon, and cardamom.

Chicken curry from the Indian subcontinent typically features chicken stewed in a tomato-based sauce seasoned with aromatic spices. This recipe, like many others, calls for curry powder (a spice blend made with coriander, turmeric, cumin, and chili powder).

Mutton Curry

Mutton curry (also referred to as kosha mangsho, lamb curry, or goat curry) is a dish that is prepared from goat meat (or sometimes lamb meat) and vegetables. The dish is found in different variations across all states, countries and regions of South Asia and the Caribbean.

Muton is a term in genetics that means the smallest unit in a chromosome that can be changed by mutations. The term Muton was created by Seymour Benzer in 1955 after his work about the mapping of bacteriophages T4.

Fish Curry

Malabar matthi curry, also known as fish curry, is an Indian and Goan dish. It consists of sardines semi-stewed in a Kerala-style curry with assorted vegetables, such as okra or onions. It is usually served with rice, naan, bread, or tapioca.

Fish curry is most prevalent in Southern and Western India, with Kerala and Goa making their versions of fish curry recipes. In Goa, fish curry is prepared with coconut milk; in Kerala, fish curry is served with rice or idli. In West Bengal, the recipe for fish curry is called Macher Jhol.

Egg Curry

Egg Curry is a spicy, delicious creamy and rich curry made by cooking boiled egg with onion, tomato masala along with herbs and spices. Egg Curry is good choice of gravy with rice, roti etc.
